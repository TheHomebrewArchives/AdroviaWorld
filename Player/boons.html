Boons

To have your character grow in power outside of your class abilities, you must take boons. Boons are special abilities or otherwise increases in power that you are able to acquire using boon points. You gain boon points both from your culture, and Boon Points column of your class table. You can spend boon points when you gain a level, during character creation, or extensive portions of downtime.

Any boons that are given by a class feature or culture do not need to have their prerequisites met.

Types of Boons

General Boons

Some boons are general, meaning that no special rules govern them as a group.

Combat Boons

Combat boons allow for you to perform feats in combat, and can be taken with the bonus boon points gained by the fighter.

Critical Boons

Critical Boons are Combat Boons that allow you to make your critical hits more devastating. You can only apply the effects of one Critical Boon to a single hit, unless you possess the Critical Mastery boon.

Spellcasting Boons

Spellcasting boons allow for more powerful and flexible magic, and can be taken with the bonus boon points gained by the sorcerer.

Metamagic Boons

Metamagic Boons are Spellcasting Boons that allow you to augment the effects of your spells. Any spellcaster can take them. You may only apply the effects of one Metamagic Boon to a given spell, unless you possess the Metamagic Mastery boon

Culture Boons

Culture boons affect special abilities granted to you by your culture. They may only be taken during character creation.

Class Boons

Class boons augment class features. You can have a maximum number of boon points allocated to Class Boons equal to your proficiency bonus + 1. You can exchange up to one Class Boon for boon points when you level up.
